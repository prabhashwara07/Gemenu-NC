History & Evolution

The Sri Padmagiri Temple, located in Kuda Nelubewa, Anuradhapura, served as the foundation for this journey. Based on unwritten evidence, it's said that from 1948 to 1952, monks residing at the temple offered education to children. On May 12, 1952, a formal school was established on privately owned land near the town of Kuda Nelubewa, backed by written documentation.

In the early days, there was no fence or formal structure—just a thatched roof made of coconut fronds. Under the guidance of the first principal, M.P. Piyasena, and with R.M. Podi Manike as the teacher, 21 children came to learn their ABCs.

As the school gradually progressed, by 1954, it had grown to 56 students studying inside a more structured building with timber walls. After receiving three decades of support and growth, the institution proudly became a fully-fledged Maha Vidyalaya (Senior School) by 1980.

A major turning point came in 1985 when it was included in the Navodaya project (a national school development initiative). Since then, the school experienced rapid physical and human development. Modern facilities like a fully equipped science lab, computer unit, library, three-storied classroom building, and an activity room stand as strong proof of this progress.

By the 1990s, the staff had grown to over 40 teachers, and student numbers surpassed 500—undeniable signs of continuous development. In 2003, being selected among 22 Navodaya schools across the island marked a proud achievement for the school.

Moreover, in a competition organized by the Northern Province Department of Education to select the best schools, this school emerged as the top performer at the zonal level.

Currently a 1C-class school under the Navodaya project, the school has had the honor of being led by 17 principals over the years, and it continues to develop rapidly. In 2012, the school earned the Excellence Award in the All-Island Productive Schools Competition, organized jointly by the Ministry of Education and DFCC Bank—yet another moment of pride.

The school has achieved numerous All-Island victories in various activities, including sports, language, and oratorical competitions, as well as other co-curricular pursuits. It is a matter of great pride that two female athletes from the school represent the Sri Lanka Under-13 Women’s Football Team. As of the start of 2021, the school entered a golden era, supported by 14 buildings and a teaching staff that has grown to 66, with a student population exceeding 1,000. The school has expanded from two parallel classes to three, and the increased enrollment in Grade 1 in 2021 reflects the growing popularity of the school within the community. This aligns with the government’s policy under the “New Dawn in School Education” initiative, which aims to develop 1,000 national schools to provide equitable, high-quality education opportunities and facilities for all Sri Lankan children. Selected at the Divisional Secretariat level, the school was designated a national school in 2021 and continues to undergo development. By the end of 2022, the teaching staff reached 75, and the student population stood at 1,329. The establishment of a distance learning resource center, a new school gate, a name board, and an improved entrance pathway have added renewed vigor, aligning the school with its vision and mission.

The current principal, W.M. Sunil Priyashantha, leads the school, supported by deputy principals W.A.I. Madhubhashini, A.M.N. Jayaratne, H.M.S.D. Wanninayake, and D.G.N.L. Deniyage. G.A.N. Karunathilaka, representing the primary section, contributes to the school’s development through participatory management. The school is now equipped with a solar panel system, a renovated office, a teacher center, and other facilities. Plans are underway to construct an outdoor stage, addressing a significant infrastructural gap, which will enhance the school’s ability to host future events.

With digitalization, the entire school is covered by Wi-Fi, facilitating educational activities for teachers. Modern smart boards, tablets, and computers have made teaching more accessible, enabling rural students to engage with advanced technology.
